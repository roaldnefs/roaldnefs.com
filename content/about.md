I'm a security and platform engineer based in the Netherlands, currently **CTO at [Warpnet](https://warpnet.nl)**. Over the past decade I've worked on security and platform engineering at organizations like [DUO](https://duo.nl/), [KPN](https://www.kpn.com/), and [CJIB](https://www.cjib.nl/).

## Community

I co-organize [BSides Groningen](https://bsidesgrunn.org/), [BSides Amsterdam](https://www.bsidesams.org/), and [Cloud Native Groningen](https://cloudnativegroningen.nl/): grassroots events for the curious, the technical, and the occasionally reckless. I also run [hands-on workshops](/training/) on automotive security and hardware hacking, and operate a Car Hacking Village at conferences across Europe.

## Open source

Most of my code lives on [GitHub](https://github.com/roaldnefs). I've contributed to projects like [SaltStack](https://github.com/saltstack/salt/pulls?q=is%3Apr+author%3Aroaldnefs), [Prometheus](https://github.com/prometheus/client_golang/pulls?q=is%3Apr+author%3Aroaldnefs), and [rfcat](https://github.com/atlas0fd00m/rfcat/pulls?q=is%3Apr+author%3Aroaldnefs), and maintain a few of my own. One of those is [WebUSB CAN](https://github.com/roaldnefs/webusb-can), the browser-based CAN console I use in my automotive workshops.

## Off the keyboard

Outside of work, automotive security and hardware hacking are the rabbit holes I keep ending up in. The [workshops](/training/) and the [Car Hacking Village](https://roaldnefs.github.io/car-hacking-village/) all come from that hobby. I also tinker with electronics, RF, and lockpicking; the [Tools](/tools/) page lists what's currently on the bench.
